1. What is the problem you are solving?
> Businesses or your audience care about problems. Make the problem clear and relatable

AI tools are now inside almost every classroom, study session, and student workflow. Institutions celebrate adoption rates. Employers demand AI literacy. Students are told, explicitly and implicitly, that Students who understand AI tools and digital systems will have a competitive advantage in the job market.(Article from Urbeuniversity)

The data says something more uncomfortable.
Students using AI the most are not necessarily performing better. They are burning out faster. And the institutions handing them access to these tools have largely done so without teaching them how to use them; leaving students in a dangerous middle ground where the tool that was supposed to help them is quietly overwhelming them instead.
The problem is not AI. The problem is unsupported AI adoption and nobody is measuring who it's breaking before it's too late.


This project asks: 
> Can we predict which students are at highest risk of AI-induced burnout; before the semester ends, before the grades drop, before they quietly disengage?

The answer matters because burnout is not just an academic problem. It is a workforce pipeline problem. The students burning out today are the professionals who will arrive in organizations tomorrow; already depleted, already disillusioned, already associating AI with pressure rather than power.
